Data Portrait

Project Information

Project by: Rachel Gorman
Link Here

Project Objective

Create a data portrait out of a personal habit of yours.

Project Description

My data portrait reflects the time (in sets of consecutive hours) that I spent either in class or on homework over the course of the week that I was tracking myself. Each column represents a day, starting with Wednesday and ending with Monday.

The white cubes represent hours (in increments of 50px)spent on homework. The black represents the hours spent in class. I chose the background color to randomize the x location as the blocks fell to reflect the constant frenetic, stressful energy you feel when work assignments begin to accumulate.

The falling motion is meant to evoke feelings of work “stacking up,” but I also wanted to visually nod to being “lost in the stacks” of the library. In the end, you can interpret the data negatively, seeing in the blocks the figure of a wall that separates me from free time, rest, or more leisurely pursuits. You might also interpret the blocks together more positively, seeing instead the beginnings of a (shaky) foundation of knowledge upon which I can build.

Personal Reflection

This data portrait project didn’t reveal anything to me about how my time is spent that I didn’t already know (though truly tracking the hours as they accumulated did take me aback). I think instead, it highlighted for me how many concepts that we’ve covered in class that I am still struggling to understand and use correctly.

One of the reasons that this project took so long to create, was because I really tried to incorporate most, or all, of the concepts we learned in class. Unfortunately, my effort failed and I had to start again with a simpler, more straightforward concept. Although I was still able to work with some ideas I hadn’t before, like frameCount and arrays, when I look at my final code I am almost positive that I could use for loops and map/millis functions to make this project more streamlined. I just have a really difficult time conceptualizing where and how they would apply. Even my if/else statements didn’t work as I intended (for some reason, I couldn’t get my else {} commands to achieve what I wanted). I am also not sure why all of my variables had to be globally defined as zero, instead of operating as intended when they were listed simply "let xpos."

This project, more than any other, has inspired me to seek extra tutoring through DT study so that I have a better handle on the basics. In that sense, I guess I gained some real insight!
